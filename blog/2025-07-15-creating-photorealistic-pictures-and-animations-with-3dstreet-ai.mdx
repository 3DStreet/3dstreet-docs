In this guide, we'll walk through the complete workflow to take a 3DStreet scene from simple 3D rendering to more realistic images and animations using Black Forest Labs and Google's Veo3 Models.

## Step 1: Create Your 3D Street Scene

First, let's create a 3D street scene like normal in 3DStreet. Whether you're starting from scratch or using a user-generated scene, make sure your layout captures the key elements of your street design vision.

*[Screenshot placeholder: 3DStreet scene creation - video timecode ~00:00:16]*

Once you're satisfied with your scene composition, it's time to export it for AI enhancement.

## Step 2: Export a Clean JPEG

Download a clean, high-resolution JPEG from your 3DStreet scene using the **share button**. This will serve as the foundation for our AI enhancement process.

*[Screenshot placeholder: Exporting JPEG from 3DStreet - video timecode ~00:00:18]*

Make sure to capture the scene from an angle that best showcases your street design, as this will be the basis for all subsequent enhancements.

## Step 3: Import to Black Forest Labs for Photorealistic Enhancement

Next, we'll import your screenshot into [Black Forest Labs](https://blackforestlabs.ai/) to add photorealistic shading and lighting. This AI-powered tool will transform your basic 3D render into a much more realistic-looking image.

*[Screenshot placeholder: Black Forest Labs interface - video timecode ~00:00:26]*

Upload your JPEG and let the AI work its magic. The system will upscale your image while adding:
- Realistic lighting and shadows
- Enhanced textures and materials
- Improved depth and atmosphere

You'll get a couple different example outputs to choose from, each with its own interpretation of how to make your scene more photorealistic.

## Step 4: Use the Fill Tool for Structural Changes

One of the most powerful features is the **Fill tool**, which allows you to modify specific parts of your scene. For example, you can change the structure of buildings to have something more realistic looking for suburban single-family homes.

*[Screenshot placeholder: Using Fill tool to modify buildings - video timecode ~00:00:39]*

The Fill tool gives you several options to choose from. You can:
- Replace generic buildings with more realistic architectural styles
- Add detailed facades and realistic building materials
- Modify landscaping and environmental elements
- Enhance street infrastructure with realistic details

*[Screenshot placeholder: Fill tool options/results - video timecode ~00:00:52]*

## Step 5: Create Animation with Google's Veo

Now comes the exciting part â€“ turning your enhanced static image into a dynamic animation! Take your photorealistic screenshot, right-click to download it, and load it into Google's Veo through their [flow tool](https://veo.google/).

Add a quick prompt like "create an animation of the suburban street scene" and let the AI process your request. The system will take about a minute to generate your animation options.

*[Screenshot placeholder: Animation generation in progress - video timecode ~00:01:09]*

## Step 6: Choose Your Animation Style

After processing, Google's Veo will show you a couple different animation options. Each will have its own interpretation of how to bring your street scene to life with:
- Subtle camera movements
- Environmental animations (trees swaying, etc.)
- Lighting changes
- Atmospheric effects

*[Screenshot placeholder: Animation options - video timecode ~00:01:15]*

You can choose which ones you like best, or sometimes you'll need to adjust your prompt slightly to get an output that perfectly matches your vision.

## Tips for Success

- **Start with a clear, well-composed 3DStreet scene** - The quality of your final animation depends heavily on your initial composition
- **Experiment with different prompts** - Small changes in your description can yield dramatically different results
- **Be patient with processing times** - AI rendering can take a few minutes, but the results are worth the wait
- **Save multiple versions** - Keep copies of your work at each stage in case you want to try different approaches
